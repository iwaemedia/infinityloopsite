APPENDIX B: HISTORICAL TIMELINE (1619 - 2025)

The Foundations: Slavery and Its Immediate Aftermath

1619 - First documented arrival of enslaved Africans in English North America (Virginia)

1662 - Virginia statute establishes that children's status (enslaved or free) follows the mother, encoding slavery into law and inheritance

1705 - Virginia Slave Codes consolidate earlier laws, establishing comprehensive legal framework for slavery

1793 - First Fugitive Slave Act passed, requiring return of escaped enslaved people

1831 - Nat Turner's Rebellion leads to intensified surveillance and control systems across the South

1850 - Fugitive Slave Act strengthened, federal enforcement of slavery expanded

1857 - Dred Scott v. Sandford: Supreme Court rules that Black people are not citizens

1863 - Emancipation Proclamation (January 1)

1865 - Thirteenth Amendment abolishes slavery except as punishment for crime

  • Black Codes enacted across Southern states
  • Freedmen's Bureau established

CHAPTER 1 ERA: RECONSTRUCTION'S VIOLENT REVERSAL (1865 - 1877)

1866 - Civil Rights Act grants citizenship (overridden by President Johnson, then passed over veto)

  • Ku Klux Klan founded in Tennessee

(1867 - 1868) - Reconstruction Acts establish military oversight of Southern states

1868 - Fourteenth Amendment grants citizenship and equal protection

1870 - Fifteenth Amendment prohibits racial discrimination in voting

1871 - Enforcement Acts (Ku Klux Klan Acts) attempt to combat terrorist violence

1873 - Colfax Massacre: White supremacists kill over 100 Black men in Louisiana

1875 - Civil Rights Act prohibits racial discrimination in public accommodations

1876 - Disputed presidential election between Hayes and Tilden

1877 - Compromise of 1877: Federal troops withdrawn from South, Reconstruction ends

CHAPTER 2 ERA: THE NADIR (1877 - 1919)

1881 - Tennessee enacts first Jim Crow law mandating railroad segregation

1883 - Supreme Court strikes down Civil Rights Act of 1875

1890 - Mississippi Plan: Constitutional convention establishes literacy tests, poll taxes to disenfranchise Black voters

1892 - Ida B. Wells begins anti-lynching journalism after Memphis lynchings

1896 - Plessy v. Ferguson: Supreme Court establishes "separate but equal" doctrine

1898 - Wilmington Massacre: White supremacists overthrow elected biracial government in North Carolina

(1900 - 1920) - Peak of lynching violence (approximately 50-100 documented lynchings annually)

1908 - Springfield Race Riot (Illinois)

1909 - NAACP founded

1915 - The Birth of a Nation film released, glorifying KKK and inspiring revival

  • Leo Frank lynching in Georgia

1917 - East St. Louis Race Riot

  • NAACP Silent Protest Parade in New York City

1919 - Red Summer: Wave of white-on-Black violence across dozens of cities

CHAPTER 3 ERA: THE GREAT MIGRATION (1919 - 1941)

1920 - UNIA (Universal Negro Improvement Association) under Marcus Garvey reaches peak membership

1921 - Tulsa Race Massacre destroys Black Wall Street

1923 - Rosewood Massacre (Florida)

1925 - A. Philip Randolph founds Brotherhood of Sleeping Car Porters

1929 - Great Depression begins, disproportionate impact on Black employment

1931 - Scottsboro Boys case begins

1933 - New Deal programs begin, many excluding Black participation or enforcing segregation

1935 - Harlem Race Riot

1938 - Missouri ex rel. Gaines v. Canada: Supreme Court orders integration of university or creation of equal facility

1941 - A. Philip Randolph threatens March on Washington, Roosevelt issues Executive Order 8802 banning discrimination in defense industries

CHAPTER 4 ERA: WORLD WAR II AND PRECURSORS (1941 - 1954)

1941 - United States enters World War II

1942 - Congress of Racial Equality (CORE) founded

1943 - Detroit Race Riot

  • Zoot Suit Riots (Los Angeles)
  • Race riots in Harlem, Mobile, Beaumont

1944 - Smith v. Allwright: Supreme Court strikes down white primaries

1945 - World War II ends

1946 - Wave of violence against returning Black veterans

1947 - To Secure These Rights report documents civil rights violations

  • Journey of Reconciliation (CORE's first Freedom Ride)

1948 - Executive Order 9981 begins military desegregation

  • Shelley v. Kraemer: Supreme Court rules racial housing covenants unenforceable

1950 - Sweatt v. Painter and McLaurin v. Oklahoma: Supreme Court challenges "separate but equal" in higher education

1954 - Brown v. Board of Education: Supreme Court declares school segregation unconstitutional

CHAPTER 5 ERA: CIVIL RIGHTS AND COINTELPRO (1954 - 1968)

1955 - Emmett Till murdered in Mississippi

  • Montgomery Bus Boycott begins

1956 - FBI begins COINTELPRO operations

1957 - Little Rock Nine integrate Central High School

  • Civil Rights Act of 1957

1960 - Greensboro sit-ins begin

  • Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee (SNCC) founded

1961 - Freedom Rides

1962 - James Meredith integrates University of Mississippi

1963 - Birmingham Campaign

  • March on Washington, "I Have a Dream" speech
  • 16th Street Baptist Church bombing kills four girls
  • Medgar Evers assassinated

1964 - Civil Rights Act prohibits discrimination

  • Mississippi Freedom Summer
  • Malcolm X leaves Nation of Islam

1965 - Malcolm X assassinated

  • Selma to Montgomery marches, "Bloody Sunday"
  • Voting Rights Act
  • Watts Rebellion (Los Angeles)

1966 - Black Panther Party founded

1967 - Long Hot Summer: Multiple urban rebellions

1968 - Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. assassinated

  • Fair Housing Act
  • Uprisings in over 100 cities following King's death

CHAPTER 6 ERA: LAW AND ORDER (1968 - 1980)

1968 - Richard Nixon elected on "law and order" platform

1969 - FBI intensifies COINTELPRO operations against Black Panthers

1970 - Comprehensive Drug Abuse Prevention and Control Act

1971 - Attica Prison Uprising

  • Nixon declares "War on Drugs"

1972 - Furman v. Georgia temporarily halts death penalty

1973 - Rockefeller Drug Laws (New York) establish mandatory minimums

1974 - Nixon resigns

1976 - Gregg v. Georgia reinstates death penalty

1978 - Regents of University of California v. Bakke limits affirmative action

CHAPTER 7 ERA: MASS INCARCERATION (1980 - 2010)

1980 - Ronald Reagan elected

1982 - War on Drugs escalated with new federal initiatives

1984 - Comprehensive Crime Control Act

  • Sentencing Reform Act creates guidelines

1986 - Anti-Drug Abuse Act creates 100:1 crack/powder cocaine sentencing disparity

1987 - McCleskey v. Kemp: Supreme Court rejects statistical evidence of death penalty racial bias

1988 - Anti-Drug Abuse Act expands mandatory minimums

1989 - Central Park Five wrongfully convicted

1991 - Rodney King beating in Los Angeles

1992 - Los Angeles Uprising following Rodney King verdict

1993 - Snoop Dogg's "Murder Was the Case" epitomizes gangsta rap era

1994 - Violent Crime Control and Law Enforcement Act (Clinton)

  • "Three strikes" federal law
  • $9.7 billion for prison construction

1996 - Prison Litigation Reform Act limits inmate lawsuits

  • Welfare Reform Act

2000 - U.S. prison population reaches 2 million

2001 - War on Terror begins, surveillance infrastructure expands

2005 - Hurricane Katrina, disproportionate impact on Black communities

2008 - Barack Obama elected, first Black president

2010 - Fair Sentencing Act reduces crack/powder disparity from 100:1 to 18:1

CHAPTER 8 ERA: DIGITAL AUGMENTATION (2010 - 2016)

2012 - Trayvon Martin killed by George Zimmerman

  • Stop-and-frisk practices challenged in Floyd v. City of New York

2013 - George Zimmerman acquitted

  • Black Lives Matter movement founded
  • Kalief Browder released after 3 years at Rikers without conviction

2014 - Michael Brown killed in Ferguson, Missouri

  • Eric Garner killed in New York City
  • Tamir Rice killed in Cleveland
  • John Crawford III killed in Ohio

2015 - DOJ Ferguson Report released (March 4)

  • Freddie Gray dies in Baltimore police custody, uprising follows
  • Sandra Bland dies in Texas jail
  • Charleston church massacre

2016 - Philando Castile killed in Minnesota

  • Alton Sterling killed in Louisiana
  • Loomis v. Wisconsin: Supreme Court allows use of COMPAS algorithm

CHAPTER 9 ERA: THE AUTOMATED LOOP (2017 - 2024)

2017 - FBI creates "Black Identity Extremist" designation (August 3)

  • Charlottesville white supremacist rally, Heather Heyer killed

2018 - Stephon Clark killed by Sacramento police

  • Carpenter v. United States: Supreme Court requires warrant for cell location data

2019 - Nijeer Parks wrongfully arrested based on facial recognition

2020 - Breonna Taylor killed in Louisville

  • George Floyd killed in Minneapolis, global protests follow
  • Robert Williams wrongfully arrested in Detroit based on facial recognition
  • Massive protests during COVID-19 pandemic

2021 - Derek Chauvin convicted of murdering George Floyd

  • Los Angeles Municipal Code 41.18 amended to criminalize homelessness
  • January 6 Capitol attack highlights disparate law enforcement response

2022 - Ketanji Brown Jackson becomes first Black woman Supreme Court Justice

2023 - Tyre Nichols killed by Memphis police

  • Facial recognition databases exceed 640 million images
  • Multiple cities ban facial recognition technology

2024 - Ongoing debates over police reform vs. abolition

  • Continued expansion of surveillance technology
  • Reparations initiatives in multiple cities

1839 - Amistad revolt

1847 - Frederick Douglass founds The North Star newspaper

1849 - Harriet Tubman escapes, begins Underground Railroad work

1857 - Dred Scott sues for freedom

(1863 - 1865) - Approximately 180,000 Black soldiers serve in Union Army

1866 - Fisk University founded (first wave of HBCUs)

1892 - Ida B. Wells begins anti-lynching campaign

1905 - Niagara Movement founded

1909 - NAACP founded

1920 - Negro National League (baseball) established

1930 - Nation of Islam founded

1942 - CORE founded

1954 - Brown v. Board litigation victory

(1955 - 1956) - Montgomery Bus Boycott

1960 - SNCC founded, sit-in movement spreads

1964 - Freedom Summer, MFDP challenge at Democratic Convention

1965 - Malcolm X assassinated, but ideology continues

1966 - Black Panther Party founded, begins survival programs

1967 - Loving v. Virginia strikes down anti-miscegenation laws

1972 - Shirley Chisholm runs for president

1989 - First reparations legislation introduced (Conyers' H.R. 40)

2013 - Black Lives Matter founded

2014 - Ferguson uprising and organizing

2016 - Movement for Black Lives policy platform released

2020 - Largest protests in U.S. history following George Floyd's murder

2021 - California becomes first state to establish reparations task force

2023 - Evanston, Illinois implements housing reparations program

(2024 - 2025) - Multiple cities consider reparations; abolitionist organizing intensifies

  • Growth of mutual aid networks
  • Reparations legislation gains momentum
  • Bans on surveillance technology spread
  • Decriminalization efforts expand

2030+ - The work continues
